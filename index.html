Creations

Thomas J. Watson Fellowship - Global Soccer Diplomacy

The Thomas J. Watson Fellowship funds an original project executed during a year of independent, purposeful exploration on a world scale. I focused on exploring unrecognized nations and communities throughout the world through a cultural and social lens as it relates to identity, political landscapes and soccer. Created Instagram account to share additional information about teams, communities and locations I visited during my Fellowship year.

Refining a Zainichi Identity with Soccer

A dive into the beginnings of the Zainichi Korean identity in Japan and their relation to the United Koreans in Japan soccer team.

Human Rights Cup Odyssey with Matabeleland Football Confederacy

The first ever Human Rights Cup in South Africa is being organized by Matabeleland Football Confederacy in Zimbabwe. This three-part story follows the Matabeleland men's and women's teams exhausting journey to Johannesburg, paired with commentary about the history of Matabeleland, Zimbabwe and identity from Director Busani Sibindi.Podcast about Matabeleland's preperation for the Human Rights Cup.

Seeking Cyprus:Mohammed and Isaac

Mohammed and Isaac, two asylum seekers on the coastal city of Larnaca, Cyprus, share their stories.

World Records Broken at Equal Playing Fields

Story about a world record breaking soccer matches promoting gender equality during the Women's World Cup in Lyon, France.
